cleaning decisions

filter years The year 2001 has a lot of missing values, we therefore decided to filter our data set to range from 2002 - 2025

invalid values When trying to standardise our data used for the algorithm we found the largest distance between two coordinate points to be ~ 600km. As we are trying to predict arrest probabilities within the city of Chicago, we decided to bound our coordinates to the city's limits. We also dropped all instances of missing coordinates.

typos in crime type The raw data included both CRIM SEXUAL ASSAULT and CRIMINAL SEXUAL ASSAULT as primary crime types. After having a closer look at the Illinois Uniform Crime Reporting (IUCR) Codes, we found no record of CRIM SEXUAL ASSAULT being an additonal crime type and therefore renamed it to match the correct spelling.

number of incidents We decided to keep crimes for which at least 5,000 incidents were reported. The variable case_number identifies every unique incident.

collapsed dataframe One incident could have various registers. We collapsed the dataframe to have one row per incident. Our label variable, arrest, indicates whether at least one person was arrested for that incident.

knn logistic ridge from scratch

our prediction We wanted to know the probability of being arrested for committing a specific crime (e.g., theft) on a given location within the City of Chicago, at a given time and date.

how we predict We use a k-nearest neighbours logistic ridge regression.

  1. We select a primary_type and, thus, discard the rest of crime types.
  2. We standardize 10 features:
    • latitude
    • longitude
    • hour_sin
    • hour_cos
    • day_of_week_sin
    • day_of_week_cos
    • month_sin
    • month_cos
    • day_of_year_sin
    • day_of_year_cos
  3. With the same parameters (mean, standard deviation), we standardise the query.
  4. For a given standardised query, we find the k (from 1 to 100) nearest neighbours.
  5. Once selected, we run a logistic ridge regression (alpha=1.0, lr=0.1, n_iter=1000) to estimate the probability of being arrested on the selected features.

Next steps

our next steps We would like to merge this dataset with other sources of information. For example, we would like add ethnic origin as an explanatory variable to determine possible biases on arrests, reported types of crimes and number of registered incidents.

what we can do better We find the k-nearest neighbours for every query. This approach might face some difficulties whith scaling: the bigger the dataframe, the longer the time of computation. We could store previous results and show an approximation in case the user introduces a very similar query.
