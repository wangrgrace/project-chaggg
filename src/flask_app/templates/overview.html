about the data

We are using a data set extracted from the Chicago Police Department's CLEAR (Citizen Law Enforcement Analysis and Reporting) system, which reflects reported incidents of crime (except murders) occurring in Chicago from 2001 to present. The data set holds updated records minus the most recent seven days. More information can be found on the City of Chicago Data Portal.

what our exploration revealed

Criminologists have long examined the spatiality and temporality of violent crime—where and when they occur, and the types of conditions that lead to them. Studies have shown associations between crime and higher temperatures (Cruz, D’Alessio, & Stolzenberg 2020), warmer seasons (Schinasi & Hamra 2017), time and day (Towers et al. 2018), and neighborhood context (Hermann 2015) in a number of major U.S. cities, noting that different crime types (e.g., theft, assault, domestic violence, etc.) follow varying trajectories. Based on these ideas, we undertook initial data exploration to illustrate crime trends in the city of Chicago.
